Welcome!

This is a live candlestick chart of Scotiabank's Toronto stock. By default, the chart focuses on data between the first day of the current month and the last day of the current month. The chart is capable of showing data from as early as 1995.

Candlesticks are useful, as they provide traders with important price points (opening, closing, high, low) in a clear and visual manner. With that price information, it becomes possible to determine potential price movements based on patterns indicated by the chart.

How to Read a Candlestick Chart

A daily candlestick represents the market's opening, closing, high, and low price for the day.

The solid colored rectangles show the range between the opening and closing prices for each day. A

green

rectangle indicates that the closing price was larger than the opening price (an increase in stock price). On the other hand, a

red

rectangle indicates that the closing price was less than the opening price (a decrease in stock price).

The thin black lines running through the middle of the colored rectangles represent the highest and lowest prices for the stock on those days. The top of the line shows the highest price, while the bottom of the line shows the lowest price.
